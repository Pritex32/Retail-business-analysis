# Retail-business-analysis

##  The business wanted answers to simple questions:
Who are my customers?
Are my sales growing or declining?
What products perform best?
When do customers buy the most?
How many types of customers do I actually have?
💡 What the data revealed shocked me

There weren’t just customers…

There were hidden customer groups inside the business.

Using clustering techniques, I identified 8 clusters, grouped into 4 major customer segments:

### 🔹 1. Premium Electronics Buyers

Clusters: 2, 7, 8

High spending customers
Major revenue drivers

👉 These are the customers you must retain at all cost.

### 🔹 2. Premium Beauty Buyers

Cluster: 4

Mostly young female customers
High-value repeat buyers

👉 Perfect for targeted campaigns and bundles.

### 🔹 3. Low-Cost Clothing Buyers

Clusters: 0, 3

Frequent buyers
Low spending per transaction

👉 Volume drivers — but need upselling strategies.

### 🔹 4. Budget / Mixed Buyers

Clusters: 1, 5, 6

Low to medium spenders
Less profitable individually

👉 Need smart promotions and product combinations.

📊 I didn’t stop there…

I built a Machine Learning model to predict how much revenue the business will generate daily.
